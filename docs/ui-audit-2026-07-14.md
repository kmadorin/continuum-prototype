# UI audit — all six seats, driven end to end

Method: the sandbox (`cd app/web && npm run dev:sandbox`) driven through three states — room
empty, deal live (price set, consented, electing, bid + both elections in), and settled — with every
seat screenshotted at each. Findings below were reproduced on screen, then traced to the line that
causes them. Nothing here is speculative.

Legend: **P0** the demo contradicts itself or fails to prove the thesis · **P1** wrong numbers or
false copy · **P2** interaction and visual.

> **Block 2 (oversight) is fixed** — finding 3. The buyer's bid and each LP's election now create their
> contentless marker (`BidFiled` / `ElectionFiled`) in the SAME transaction as the private contract, and the
> GP's deal page counts markers instead of polling for contracts it can never see. Verified by clicking the
> real UI in a browser: the GP reads "2 of 2 responded" and "1 sealed bid filed" while its projection still
> contains zero `SealedBid` and zero `LPElection`.
>
> **Block 1 (the money) is fixed** — findings 1, 2, 5, 6 below, plus a `cost` double-count found while
> fixing them. The cap table now composes: two LP positions ($300M selling + $200M rolling) that sum to
> the $500M reference NAV, three settlement legs (288M units → buyer, 192M units → roller, $288M cash →
> seller) whose unit legs sum to the $480M PSA price, and both old-fund interests burned. Verified end to
> end in the sandbox: every seat is now paid exactly what its own screen promised. The rest stands.

## P0 — the deal tells different stories to different seats

### 1. ~~The exiting LP is promised $96.0M and paid $460.8M~~ — FIXED
Its own screen, pre-close: *"IF YOU SELL → $96.0M cash"* (stake $100.0M × 96%). Post-close, the same
screen: *"YOUR OUTCOME +460,800,000 USDC"*, *"PROCEEDS RECEIVED $460.8M"*. A 4.8× discrepancy on the
one number that seat exists to see.

Cause: `DEMO.cashAmt = '460800000.0'` (`lib/useLedger.ts`) is 96% of the **whole PSA** ($480M), but it
is paid to a single LP whose position is $100M. The demo's cap table does not compose: `refNav` is
$500M, yet the only modelled positions are two LPs at $100M each. Either the LP's stake is the whole
sell side, or the cash leg is wrong — pick one and make every screen agree.

### 2. ~~The rolling LP is a dead end, and the close lies to it~~ — FIXED
`Advisor.tsx` only ever proposes an `ExecDelegationProposal` to the buyer and the exiting LP, and only
ever allocates two legs (`unit-buyer`, `cash-lp`). So the rolling LP:
- can never press *"Accept execution delegation"* — permanently *"Waiting on the GP's delegation proposal"*;
- receives nothing at Close — *"My rolled holding — NONE YET"* stays after settlement;
- yet its SETTLED overlay announces *"Your position rolled into the new vehicle — no cash leg."*

One of the two investor archetypes in the pitch never actually rolls. Fixing it means a third leg
(rolled units) plus a delegation to that seat — and the leg amounts must still sum to `psaPrice`, so
this and finding 1 have to be solved together as one set of numbers.

### 3. ~~The GP is permanently blind to the auction and the elections~~ — FIXED
The Deal Page reads `SealedBid` and `LPElection` from the **GP's own** projection (`DealPage.tsx:171-172`)
— contracts whose only signatory is the buyer / the LP. They can never appear there. Result: *"ELECTIONS
0 of 2 responded"* forever, even with both elections filed, and no bid is ever counted or fed into
Recent Activity.

This is the product's central claim ("private inputs, aggregate oversight") failing in the UI. The
contracts already ship the answer — `Auction:BidFiled` and `Election:ElectionFiled` are contentless
markers observed by the GP — and **nothing in the frontend ever creates them**. Frontend-only fix:
create the marker in the same submit as the bid / the election, and count markers, not contents.

### 4. The LPAC cannot see the valuation it is supposed to validate — DEFERRED (open decision)

> **Deliberately not fixed.** The only fix is a Daml change (`observer gp` → `observer gp, lpac` on
> `ValuationReport`), which means rebuilding `continuum-contracts` and redeploying to devnet — a governance
> call, not a UI one, so it is parked for the contract owner. **Until it is made, the LPAC's review queue and
> its `/verify/valuation-report` are theatre**: they will keep reporting the anchored Kroll valuation as
> "NOT YET ANCHORED" no matter what the frontend does, because the LPAC is not a stakeholder of that
> contract. Finding 9 (the valuer's copy claiming the LPAC verifies the document) stays true only if this is
> fixed; if the decision is to leave the projection as-is, the LPAC's valuation row should be relabelled to
> say plainly that the document is not in its projection, rather than implying it has not been signed.

LPAC review queue: *"valuation-report · NOT YET ANCHORED"*; Documents: *"Independent Valuation —
Awaiting · Draft"* — while Kroll's report is anchored on-ledger and the GP sees it fine.

Cause is in Daml, not the UI: `Valuation.daml` declares `signatory agent, observer gp` — the LPAC is not
a stakeholder, so `/verify/valuation-report` (which reads the **session party's** ACS) can never match for
it. The governance seat is structurally blind to the document its fairness opinion is supposed to rest
on. Needs a contract change (add `lpac` to the observers) — or the LPAC's review queue is theatre.

## P1 — wrong numbers, false copy

5. ~~**The clearing-price tile means something different on every screen.**~~ — FIXED. `FocusedPage.tsx:84` computes the
   sub-line as `LP_POSITION_NAV × pct` — i.e. `$100M × 0.96 = $96.0M` — and shows it to **every** narrow seat,
   including the buyer and the LPAC, for whom "an LP's position" is meaningless. The GP's tile shows the real
   deal-level figure, `$480.0M`. Same label, same styling, two different quantities.

6. ~~**Sell-vs-Roll rolls at the wrong price.**~~ — FIXED. `SellVsRoll.tsx`: `const units = stakeNav` → *"~100,000,000 CV
   units @ $1.00"* for a $100M position, under a header that reads *"same 96%-of-independent-NAV basis"*.
   `Clearing.daml` mints `roundDollar (clearing × rollNav)` = **96,000,000**. The panel whose entire job is the
   sell-vs-roll decision overstates the roll by 4%.

7. **The deal has two names.** — DEFERRED (open decision). The vehicle is `Meridian CV I` on-ledger and
   *"Project Continuum CV I, L.P."* in the three anchored documents and the hardcoded `<h1>`. Picking the
   ledger's name means retitling the documents and regenerating their sha256 (there is a generator,
   `custody/docs/gen-manifest.ts`), which invalidates the anchor already committed on devnet until the next
   "Reset demo". Picking the documents' name means renaming the deal in the backend's epoch keys instead.
   Parked by decision; the header still shows the hardcoded name.
   Original finding: `<h1>Project Continuum CV I, L.P.</h1>` is hardcoded in `DealPage.tsx:266` and
   `FocusedPage.tsx:179`, while the on-ledger `cv` is *"Meridian CV I"* — which is what the settled overlay and
   the activity feed print. Both names appear on the same screen.

8. ~~**The sponsor is the custodian.**~~ — FIXED. The Deal Page prints *"Sponsor: Fireblocks — GP Treasury"* — Fireblocks is
   the custodian that holds the key, not the sponsor. Sign-in calls the same seat *"Dana Whitfield · Whitfield
   Advisory"*.

9. ~~**The valuer's copy overpromises.**~~ — FIXED. *"The GP, buyer, LPs and LPAC each verify against the served document"* —
   per finding 4, only the GP can.

## P2 — interaction and visual

10. ~~**The SETTLED overlay is an inescapable takeover.**~~ — FIXED. `role="dialog"`, no dismiss, no Esc (`Settlement.tsx:149`).
    After the climax you cannot get back to the deal — and the Ledger Inspector, the "this is not a mock" proof,
    sits behind it. Reload or sign out are the only exits.
11. ~~**Post-close affordances stay live.**~~ — FIXED. The exiting LP's *"Propose participation"* is still enabled after
    settlement, next to *"Waiting on the GP's old-fund interest offer."*
12. ~~**The GP page shows the same stepper twice**~~ — FIXED. — the top stepper and the "Lifecycle" card are identical.
13. ~~**"What happens next" goes stale**~~ — FIXED.: it said *"Open elections…"* while the deal was already `Electing`.
14. **Active and done steps look alike.** The rolling LP's stepper highlights *"Closed"* (as the current step)
    while the strip below reads *"LIVE · AWAITING ATOMIC SETTLEMENT"*.
15. ~~**Privacy guarantees are printed in the failure colour.**~~ — FIXED. *"No other buyer — and not the GP — can see this…"*,
    *"Peer-blind: no other LP's projection contains this election."* — red reads as an error; these are the
    product working.
16. **The activity feed never records the close.** After settlement its newest entry is still "Clearing price set".
17. ~~**Sign-in has an empty grid cell**~~ — FIXED. next to Kroll.
18. **Dead branch:** nothing ever creates an `LPACConsentRequest`, so the approval queue's `Grant` path is
    unreachable (`ApprovalQueue.tsx:155`).
19. ~~**Stale deal cid.**~~ — FIXED. The GP caches the deal's `contractId`; the LPAC's `RecordConsent` is consuming and
    replaces it. A GP that acts without refreshing submits against an archived contract.
20. **Layout:** every narrow seat has a screen of dead vertical space, and the trust-model footer is an
    undifferentiated wall of mono text on all six.

## Fixed in blocks 4–5 (copy + interaction)

- **8** — the sponsor line named Fireblocks, which is the CUSTODIAN holding the key. It now names the
  advisory firm; the custodian stays in the topbar badge where it belongs.
- **9** — the valuer no longer claims the buyer, the LPs and the LPAC verify its document. Only the GP can
  (see finding 4). A seat whose whole job is verifiable anchoring must not overstate its own audience.
- **10** — the SETTLED takeover dismisses on Esc, on the ✕, or on a backdrop click, and leaves a persistent
  "Settled · atomic close" strip with a "View settlement" button. The proof it used to hide — the ledger
  tree, the audit trail, the settled holding — is reachable again. A proof you cannot reach is not a proof.
- **11** — post-close, both LPs' pre-authorization card says "consumed by the close" instead of offering
  buttons whose contracts the Close has already spent, and claiming to be "waiting on the GP".
- **12** — the duplicated "Lifecycle" stepper is gone; the header keeps the one stepper.
- **13** — the GP's next-step line no longer says "open elections" while elections are open.
- **15** — privacy guarantees are no longer painted in the failure colour. They are the product working.
- **17** — the sixth sign-in cell is filled: the independent valuer gets a full-width card, like the GP.
  The conflicted organizer and the independent check on it are the two seats that shape the story.
- **19** — the GP re-reads the deal's contract id at the moment it acts. Deal choices are consuming, and the
  LPAC's consent (another session) replaces the contract mid-flight; the cached id would fail with an error
  that looked unrelated to the click.

Not fixed: **14** (active vs done steps look alike), **16** (activity feed's close entry), **18** (the
`LPACConsentRequest` → `Grant` path is still unreachable — now that `BidFiled` markers exist, the recusal
check it was built for would actually work), **20** (dead vertical space, the mono-wall trust footer).

## Fixed in block 2

- **3** — oversight runs on the markers the contracts already shipped. The GP counts WHO responded; the
  amounts stay in contracts it is not a stakeholder of. The activity feed says "filed a sealed bid — amount
  blind to you" instead of silently printing nothing. The LPAC sees the bid markers too, which is what
  `Consent.Grant`'s recusal check needs (finding 18).

## Fixed in block 1

- **1** — the cap table now composes; the seller is paid the $288M its screen quotes.
- **2** — the GP proposes a delegation and an old-fund interest to the rolling LP and allocates a third
  leg (192M rolled units); the roller accepts both and proposes participation, so the close burns its old
  position too. The SETTLED overlay's promise is now true.
- **5** — the clearing-price tile shows the deal-level purchase price on every seat. Personal figures live
  in "My position" and the sell-vs-roll panel, where they belong.
- **6** — `SellVsRoll` reprices the roll at the clearing price (192M units for a $200M stake), matching
  `Clearing.daml`.
- **new** — `HoldingReceipt` multiplied the unit count by the clearing price to get "cost", double-counting
  the discount (units are issued at $1.00; the discount is already in how many you get).

## Suggested order

1. **Fix the money first** (1 + 2 + 6): settle one coherent set of numbers — positions, sell leg, roll leg,
   buyer leg summing to `psaPrice` — and make every seat's screens derive from it rather than from hardcoded
   `DEMO` constants. This is also the only way the rolling-LP seat becomes real.
2. **Make oversight work** (3): create `BidFiled` / `ElectionFiled` alongside the private contracts, and count
   markers. Frontend-only, and it turns the deal page from "0 of 2" into the actual privacy story.
3. **Decide on the LPAC's sight line** (4): a one-line Daml change, but it is a governance decision, not a
   styling one.
4. Then the copy (5, 7, 8, 9) and the interaction pass (10–20).
