Kroll Valuation Services ("Kroll") has been retained by the Sponsor as an independent valuation agent to opine on the fair value of the limited-partnership interests in Meridian Growth Fund III proposed for transfer into Project Continuum CV I, L.P. (the "Continuation Vehicle"). Our engagement is independent of the Sponsor and of the LP Advisory Committee, and our compensation is not contingent on the conclusions reached herein.

Net asset value of the transferring interests as of 30 June 2026. Concluded fair value: $500.0 million, within a reasonable range of $480.0M – $520.0M.

Methodology

Our conclusion is supported by two independent approaches, cross-checked for consistency. Under the income approach we constructed a discounted-cash-flow model of the portfolio's projected distributions over a five-year horizon, discounted at a risk-adjusted rate of 14.5% reflecting the illiquidity and stage of the underlying assets, with a terminal value derived from a normalized exit multiple. Under the market approach we applied trading and transaction multiples (EV/EBITDA and EV/Revenue) drawn from a set of comparable public companies and recent precedent secondary transactions, adjusted for size, growth and control. The two approaches produced overlapping ranges; we weighted them 60/40 in favour of the income approach given the quality of the underlying cash-flow forecasts.

Fair-Value Opinion

Based upon and subject to the assumptions, procedures and limiting conditions set out in this report, it is our opinion that the fair value of the transferring limited-partnership interests as of 30 June 2026 is reasonably stated at $500.0 million, within a range of $480.0 million to $520.0 million. A clearing price struck within this range would, in our view, be consistent with fair value to the transferring and continuing limited partners.
